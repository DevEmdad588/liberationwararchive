Interviews

Oral Histories & Interviews

Firsthand testimonies from freedom fighters and witnesses of the 1971 Liberation War.

Abdul Karim
  • Born: 1942, Comilla
  • Role: Sector 2 Freedom Fighter
  • Interview Date: March 12, 2023

Abdul Karim recounts his early involvement in the resistance following the March 25 crackdown. He describes guerrilla training, border crossings into India, and coordinated attacks on Pakistani military supply routes. His testimony highlights the hardships faced by rural fighters and the strong sense of unity among civilians.

Rahima Begum
  • Born: 1955, Khulna
  • Role: Civilian War Witness
  • Interview Date: July 5, 2022

Rahima Begum shares her memories as a young girl during the war, including displacement, food shortages, and witnessing violence in her village. Her interview focuses on the experiences of women and children and the long-term psychological impact of the conflict.

Lt. Col. (Retd.) Fazlur Rahman
  • Born: 1938, Mymensingh
  • Role: Mukti Bahini Commander
  • Interview Date: January 18, 2024

Lt. Col. Fazlur Rahman provides strategic insights into sector-based operations, coordination with Indian forces, and challenges of command during the final months of the war. His testimony sheds light on leadership decisions and battlefield realities.

Dr. Shamsun Nahar
  • Born: 1946, Barisal
  • Role: Field Medical Volunteer
  • Interview Date: November 2, 2023

Dr. Shamsun Nahar describes providing emergency medical care in makeshift camps near the border. She discusses shortages of supplies, treating wounded fighters, and the ethical challenges faced by volunteer doctors during the war.
